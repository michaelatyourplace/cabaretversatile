What is Burlesque?

An informative guide to the art of the tease and how to get started.

Burlesque is a theatrical art form that combines comedy, satire, and striptease. It's often misunderstood as simply stripping, but at its heart, it's about the "art of the tease"—a playful, glamorous, and often humorous performance that celebrates self-confidence and body positivity.

A burlesque performance can be anything from a classic feather fan dance to a high-energy routine set to a pop song. Performers use costumes, props, and facial expressions to tell a story and engage with the audience, making the removal of clothing a theatrical climax rather than the main event.

A Brief History

The term "burlesque" originated in 19th-century Europe, referring to a literary or dramatic work that makes fun of serious subjects. Over time, it evolved into a popular form of variety entertainment in America, where female performers would use comedy and song to poke fun at the societal norms of the time.

Its golden age was in the early 20th century, but it declined in popularity in the mid-1900s. A modern neo-burlesque revival began in the 1990s, led by performers like Dita Von Teese, which brought the art form back to the mainstream and expanded its reach to a diverse new audience.

How to Get Started

If you're interested in performing or simply want to explore this expressive art form, there are many ways to start learning. Burlesque is an inclusive community that welcomes people of all shapes, sizes, and genders.

1. In-Person Classes

The most popular way to learn is by taking local classes. Many dance studios and specialized schools offer beginner-friendly burlesque courses.

  • What to expect: Classes typically focus on fundamental movements, walking techniques, glove peels, and veil work. You'll learn short choreographed routines and develop your stage presence in a safe and supportive environment.
  • Where to look: Do a search for "burlesque classes near me" or "burlesque dance studio" in your area.

2. Online Courses and Workshops

If in-person classes aren't an option, or you prefer to learn at your own pace, the internet offers a wealth of resources.

  • What to expect: Online platforms provide structured lessons, from basic moves to more advanced choreography and character development. You can learn from top performers around the world.
  • Where to look: Many professional performers and burlesque schools have their own websites with on-demand courses or live-streamed classes.

3. Workshops at Festivals

For a more intensive learning experience, consider attending a burlesque festival or a specific workshop.

  • What to expect: These events often feature classes on specialized skills like tassel twirling, prop work (e.g., fan dancing), costume construction, or character creation. They are also a great way to network with other performers.
  • Where to look: Keep an eye out for major events like The Burlesque Hall of Fame Weekend in Las Vegas or regional festivals in your area.

Key Skills to Develop

Regardless of how you choose to learn, some of the most important skills in burlesque are not just about dancing. They include:

  • Stage Presence: The ability to command attention and connect with the audience.
  • Musicality: Understanding rhythm and expressing emotions through movement.
  • Character Development: Creating a persona that is unique and engaging.
  • Costuming: The art of crafting a reveal that is both glamorous and impactful.

Enjoy the journey of self-discovery and fun that burlesque has to offer!
